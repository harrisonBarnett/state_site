Soda City


Columbia, the capital of South Carolina, holds the distinction of being the state's second-most populous city, with 136,632 residents as of the 2020 census. Serving as the county seat of Richland County, it also spills over into parts of neighboring Lexington County.


Columbia stands at the heart of the Columbia, SC Metropolitan Statistical Area, boasting a population of 829,470 in 2020. It ranks as the seventh most populous urban center in the Deep South and the seventy-second most populous metropolitan statistical area in the nation. The name "Columbia" carries a poetic resonance, originating from Christopher Columbus, the explorer under the Spanish Crown, and symbolically representing the United States.
